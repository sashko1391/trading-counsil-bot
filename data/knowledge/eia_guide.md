# Guide to Interpreting EIA Weekly Petroleum Status Report

How to read the EIA Weekly Petroleum Status Report (released every Wednesday at 10:30 AM ET).

---

## Key Metrics

### Crude Oil Inventories (Commercial Stocks)
- What: total US commercial crude oil stocks excluding SPR
- Current baseline: ~420-440 million barrels (as of 2024)
- Reported as: absolute level and weekly change (draw or build)
- Most market-moving single data point in the report

### Crude Oil Production
- What: US domestic crude oil production estimate
- Current level: ~13.0-13.3 million barrels per day (mbd)
- Reported weekly but often revised; monthly estimates more reliable
- Rising production is bearish; declining production is bullish

### Crude Oil Imports / Exports
- Net imports = gross imports minus exports
- US is now a net exporter of petroleum products
- Crude imports: ~6-7 mbd; crude exports: ~4-5 mbd
- Spike in imports -> likely inventory build; drop in imports -> potential draw

### Refinery Utilization Rate
- What: percentage of refinery capacity in use (operable capacity)
- Normal range: 85-95% depending on season
- Peak: 93-96% during summer driving season (July-August)
- Trough: 82-88% during spring turnaround season (February-April)

### Gasoline Inventories
- Seasonal peak: late winter / early spring (pre-driving season)
- Seasonal trough: September-October (post-driving season)
- Below 5-year average: bullish for gasoline cracks and supportive for crude
- Key driver for retail gasoline prices and political sensitivity

### Distillate Inventories (Diesel + Heating Oil)
- More critical for oil markets than gasoline in winter months
- Below 5-year average: very bullish for Gasoil crack spreads
- Tight East Coast (PADD 1) distillate stocks -> heating oil price spikes
- Watch absolute level relative to 5-year range and days of supply

---

## Bullish vs Bearish Readings

### Bullish signals (price supportive)
- Crude stock draw > 3 million barrels (larger than consensus)
- Crude stock draw with declining imports (organic tightening)
- Refinery utilization above 93% (maximum product demand for crude)
- Distillate stocks below 5-year average by > 10%
- Production flat or declining week-over-week
- Gasoline demand (implied) above 9.5 mbd (strong consumer demand)
- Cushing OK hub stocks below 25 million barrels (pipeline operational minimum)

### Bearish signals (price negative)
- Crude stock build > 3 million barrels (larger than consensus)
- Crude stock build with rising imports (oversupply)
- Refinery utilization below 88% outside turnaround season (demand weakness)
- Distillate stocks above 5-year average by > 10%
- Production rising week-over-week
- Gasoline demand below 8.5 mbd (weak consumer demand)
- Cushing stocks above 40 million barrels (approaching tank tops)

### Neutral / mixed signals
- Inventory change within +/- 1 million barrels of consensus
- Seasonal inventory moves in line with historical patterns
- Refinery utilization moving in expected seasonal direction

## Historical Context for Draw/Build Sizes

### Crude oil weekly changes
- Average weekly build: +1 to +2 mb (during injection season, Q4-Q1)
- Average weekly draw: -1 to -3 mb (during draw season, Q2-Q3)
- Large build: > +5 mb (bearish, especially outside injection season)
- Large draw: > -5 mb (bullish, especially outside draw season)
- Extreme: > +/- 10 mb (rare, usually weather or disruption related)
- Largest historical draw: -17.1 mb (September 2017, Hurricane Harvey)

### Cushing, Oklahoma hub stocks
- Key delivery point for WTI futures
- Operational minimum: ~20-22 mb (below this, pipeline scheduling constrained)
- Comfortable range: 25-35 mb
- Tank tops: ~76 mb capacity; above 60 mb is very bearish for WTI
- Cushing approaching operational minimum -> strong WTI backwardation

### Gasoline seasonal norms
- Summer peak demand: 9.0-9.8 mbd (implied demand, 4-week average)
- Winter demand: 8.0-8.8 mbd
- Stocks seasonal peak: ~250 mb (February-March)
- Stocks seasonal trough: ~205-215 mb (September-October)

### Distillate seasonal norms
- Winter peak demand: 4.0-4.5 mbd
- Summer demand: 3.5-4.0 mbd
- Stocks seasonal peak: ~155-170 mb (October, pre-winter build)
- Stocks seasonal trough: ~115-130 mb (March-April, post-winter)

## Trading the EIA Report

### Pre-report positioning
- API (American Petroleum Institute) report released Tuesday evening provides preview
- API and EIA typically agree on direction 70-80% of the time
- Large API/EIA divergence creates volatility (market re-prices on EIA as "official")
- Consensus estimates from analyst surveys published pre-report (Reuters, Bloomberg)

### Market reaction timing
- Initial reaction: within 1-2 minutes of release (algorithmic trading)
- Secondary reaction: 10-30 minutes as traders digest full report details
- Product-specific moves: gasoline and distillate components often matter more than headline crude
- Cross-market: USD/CAD, energy equities also react

### What the market focuses on most
1. Headline crude stock change vs consensus (most important)
2. Cushing stock change (affects WTI specifically)
3. Gasoline demand (implied) — consumer health proxy
4. Distillate stocks vs 5-year average — heating/industrial health
5. Refinery utilization — crude demand proxy
6. Production estimates — supply outlook
