# China Oil Demand Guide

---

## China as Oil Consumer

China is the world's largest crude oil importer and #2 consumer (after US):
- Imports: ~11-12 million bpd (2024)
- Domestic production: ~4.2 mbpd
- Total consumption: ~16 mbpd
- Import dependency: ~73%
- Strategic Petroleum Reserve: estimated 600-900 million barrels

---

## Key Demand Indicators

**Manufacturing PMI (Caixin + NBS):**
- Above 50 = expansion = bullish for oil demand
- Below 50 = contraction = bearish
- Published: 1st of each month (NBS) and ~2nd business day (Caixin)
- NBS PMI is more comprehensive; Caixin focuses on smaller firms
- PMI move from 49→51 typically correlates with +200-400k bpd demand uptick over 3 months

**Teapot Refinery Utilization:**
- Independent ("teapot") refineries in Shandong province process ~4 mbpd
- Utilization rates signal real-time demand: >70% = strong, <60% = weak
- Teapots are marginal buyers — they increase imports when margins are good

**Property Sector:**
- Construction is diesel-intensive (cement, steel, transport)
- Property downturn (2022-2025) has been a demand headwind
- New property starts are a leading indicator for diesel demand (3-6 month lag)

---

## SPR Filling Patterns

China opportunistically fills its SPR when prices are low:
- Major filling: Q2-Q3 2020 ($20-40/bbl), early 2023 ($70-75/bbl)
- Estimated SPR capacity: ~1.5 billion barrels (target 90 days of import cover)
- SPR buying creates a floor under prices during downturns
- Signal: elevated Chinese crude imports without corresponding refinery throughput increase = SPR filling

---

## Seasonal Patterns

- **Chinese New Year (Jan/Feb):** Reduced industrial activity, lower crude runs. Travel demand spike (gasoline/jet fuel).
- **March-May:** Industrial ramp-up post-holiday. Refinery maintenance season.
- **June-August:** Peak driving season. Air conditioning demand (power generation).
- **September-October:** Golden Week (Oct 1-7) travel peak. Pre-winter diesel stocking.
- **November-December:** Heating season in Northern China. Quota allocations for next year announced.

---

## Import Quotas

China controls oil product imports/exports through quota system:
- Crude import quotas issued to teapots (annual allowances)
- Product export quotas control refined product outflows
- Quota issuance pace signals government demand outlook
- Large quota batches = government expects strong demand / wants to suppress domestic prices
- Reduced quotas = demand concerns or environmental restrictions

---

## India as Comparison (3rd Largest Importer)

India complements China demand analysis:
- Imports: ~5 mbpd, growing ~200-300k bpd annually
- Fastest-growing major oil consumer
- PPAC monthly data (25th of month) tracks imports/consumption
- India heavily imports Russian crude (discount advantage)
- Monsoon season (Jun-Sep) reduces diesel demand (agriculture/transport disruption)
