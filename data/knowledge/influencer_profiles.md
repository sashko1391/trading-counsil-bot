# Oil Market Influencer Profiles

---

## Tier 1: Leading Indicators (tweet before/during price moves)

### @JavierBlas — Bloomberg (Weight: 0.90)
Senior energy correspondent at Bloomberg. Former FT commodities editor.
- Covers: OPEC decisions, oil trade flows, energy policy, geopolitics
- Signal type: Breaking news, insider OPEC intel, market-moving commentary
- Reliability: Very high. Well-sourced within OPEC+ delegations
- Typical lead time: 30-120 minutes before official announcements
- Red flag: When he tweets about "unusual tanker movements" — usually precedes sanctions news

### @Amena_Bakr — Energy Intelligence (Weight: 0.92)
Chief OPEC correspondent at Energy Intelligence.
- Covers: OPEC+ internal dynamics, quota decisions, compliance
- Signal type: Real-time OPEC meeting updates, delegate quotes
- Reliability: Highest for OPEC-specific news. Direct access to delegates
- Typical lead time: During meetings, she's often first to report outcomes
- Her "sources say" tweets have very high hit rate

### @DavidSheppard_ — Financial Times (Weight: 0.85)
Energy editor at FT.
- Covers: Broad energy markets, gas/LNG, oil market structure
- Signal type: Market analysis, breaking news, long-form context
- Reliability: High for European energy perspective
- Particularly valuable during European energy crises

### @AlexLongley1 — Bloomberg (Weight: 0.82)
Bloomberg oil reporter.
- Covers: Physical oil markets, tanker flows, storage
- Signal type: Data-driven observations, physical market signals
- Reliability: High for physical market dynamics

### @summer_said — WSJ (Weight: 0.88)
Energy reporter at Wall Street Journal.
- Covers: Saudi Arabia energy policy, OPEC+, Middle East oil
- Signal type: Exclusive Saudi policy signals, OPEC strategy
- Reliability: Very high for Saudi-specific intel
- Her exclusives often move markets

## Tier 2: Data Providers (leading for flows/positioning)

### @TankerTrackers (Weight: 0.80)
Independent tanker tracking service.
- Covers: Global oil shipment tracking via satellite/AIS data
- Signal type: Real-time export volumes, sanctions evasion detection
- Reliability: High for physical flow data
- Key signals: Iranian/Venezuelan export changes, floating storage levels

### @Kpler (Weight: 0.82)
Commodity data analytics firm.
- Covers: Seaborne oil flows, storage, refinery intake
- Signal type: Data snapshots, flow analysis, inventory estimates
- Reliability: High. Institutional-grade data
- Their weekly flow estimates often preview official data

### @Vortexa (Weight: 0.80)
AI-powered cargo tracking.
- Covers: Global oil movements, floating storage, trade flows
- Signal type: Real-time cargo tracking, storage estimates
- Reliability: High. Complementary to Kpler
- Particularly strong on floating storage metrics
