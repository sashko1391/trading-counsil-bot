# US Shale Oil Economics

---

## Breakeven Prices

US shale production is the world's swing supply. Breakeven prices determine the floor for investment:

| Basin | Breakeven ($/bbl) | Peak Production | Notes |
|-------|-------------------|-----------------|-------|
| Permian (Delaware) | $40-50 | ~6.0 mbpd | Lowest cost, largest basin |
| Permian (Midland) | $45-55 | Included above | More mature, higher decline rates |
| Eagle Ford (TX) | $50-60 | ~1.2 mbpd | Condensate-rich, declining inventory |
| Bakken (ND) | $55-65 | ~1.2 mbpd | Light sweet, winter operational challenges |
| DJ Basin (Niobrara) | $50-58 | ~0.6 mbpd | Regulatory risk (Colorado) |
| Anadarko (SCOOP/STACK) | $55-65 | ~0.4 mbpd | Mature, limited new investment |

All-in breakevens (including land costs, G&A, dividends): $55-70/bbl for most producers.
Half-cycle breakevens (drilling + completion only): $35-50/bbl.

## DUC Wells (Drilled but Uncompleted)

DUC wells are a leading indicator of near-term supply response:
- High DUC count = supply can ramp quickly without new drilling
- DUC count has declined from 8,800 (2020) to ~4,500 (2024) — buffer is shrinking
- Completing a DUC takes 2-4 weeks vs 3-6 months for new drilling
- DUC drawdown = producers are completing existing wells rather than drilling new ones (capital discipline)

## Rig Count Interpretation

Baker Hughes weekly rig count is the primary drilling activity indicator:

- **Rising rig count:** Producers responding to higher prices. Supply increase in 6-9 months.
- **Falling rig count:** Producers pulling back. Supply decrease in 6-9 months.
- **Flat rig count + rising production:** Efficiency gains (longer laterals, better completion techniques)
- **Current regime (2024-2026):** Capital discipline — producers prioritize returns over growth

Key levels:
- 500+ rigs: Growth mode (production increasing)
- 400-500 rigs: Maintenance mode (production flat)
- Below 400 rigs: Decline mode (production falling)
- 2020 trough: 172 rigs (COVID)

## Shale Response Lag

The time from price signal to production response:
- Price rises → rig deployment: 1-3 months
- Drilling to completion: 2-4 months
- Completion to first production: 1-2 months
- **Total lag: 6-9 months** from price signal to meaningful supply change

This lag creates cyclical overshoot/undershoot patterns in oil markets.

---

## Decline Rates

Shale wells have steep decline curves:
- Year 1: 60-70% decline from initial production (IP)
- Year 2: 30-40% decline
- Year 3+: 10-15% annual decline
- **Legacy decline:** The existing base production drops ~300-500k bpd per month if no new wells drilled

This means US shale needs constant new drilling just to maintain current production (treadmill effect).

---

## Capital Discipline Era (2020+)

Post-COVID, shale producers shifted from "growth at any cost" to "returns-focused":
- Reinvestment rate: dropped from 130%+ (2018) to 50-70% (2024)
- Free cash flow → dividends + buybacks, not new drilling
- PE/institutional pressure against production growth
- Result: slower supply response to price signals than 2015-2019 era
- Implication: bullish long-term, as spare capacity response is slower
