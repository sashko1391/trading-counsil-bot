# OPEC+ Decisions and Market Reactions (2019-2024)

Historical reference for understanding OPEC+ decision patterns and their market impact.

---

## 2024 Key Decisions

### June 2024 — Gradual unwinding plan
- Decision: extend deep cuts through Q3, begin gradual unwinding from Q4 2024
- Voluntary cuts of 2.2 mbd extended; plan to phase out over 12 months
- Market reaction: Brent dropped ~$3 on perceived dovish tilt
- Lesson: market focuses on future supply, not current compliance

### March 2024 — Voluntary cuts extended
- Decision: extend 2.2 mbd voluntary cuts through Q2 2024
- Saudi Arabia maintained 1 mbd unilateral cut
- Market reaction: muted, largely priced in
- Lesson: expected extensions get minimal price reaction

---

## 2023 Key Decisions

### November 2023 — Voluntary cuts deepened
- Decision: additional 2.2 mbd voluntary cuts for Q1 2024
- Led by Saudi Arabia (1 mbd), Russia (500 kbd), plus Iraq, UAE, Kuwait
- Market reaction: Brent initially rose $2, then gave back gains on scepticism
- Lesson: "voluntary" cuts face credibility discount vs mandatory cuts

### June 2023 — Saudi unilateral cut
- Decision: Saudi Arabia adds 1 mbd unilateral cut (total to ~10 mbd)
- Effective July 2023, extended monthly through year-end
- Market reaction: Brent rose $1.50, then trended higher to $95 by September
- Lesson: Saudi unilateral action seen as more credible than group decisions

### April 2023 — Surprise OPEC+ cuts
- Decision: 1.66 mbd surprise voluntary cuts announced on a Sunday
- Led by Saudi (500 kbd), Iraq (211 kbd), UAE (144 kbd), Kuwait (128 kbd)
- Market reaction: Brent gapped up $6 on Monday open
- Lesson: surprise weekend announcements create largest price gaps

## 2022 Key Decisions

### October 2022 — Major production cut
- Decision: 2 mbd cut (largest since COVID) amid recession fears
- US publicly criticized the decision as politically motivated
- Market reaction: Brent rose $2 initially, then ranged $85-95
- Lesson: large cuts in a weakening demand environment limit upside

### August 2022 — Token increase
- Decision: 100 kbd increase for September (smallest ever OPEC+ adjustment)
- Seen as a rebuff to US pressure for faster output increases
- Market reaction: Brent rose $2 on perceived supply discipline

---

## 2021 Key Decisions

### July 2021 — Gradual unwinding of COVID cuts
- Decision: monthly 400 kbd increases starting August 2021
- UAE dispute delayed agreement briefly (demanded higher baseline)
- Market reaction: initially bearish, then bullish as demand outpaced supply additions

### April 2021 — Phased return of production
- Decision: gradual 3-phase production increase May-July 2021
- Market was recovering; OPEC+ cautiously optimistic
- Market reaction: Brent steadily climbed from $65 to $75

---

## 2020 Key Decisions

### April 2020 — Historic 9.7 mbd cut
- Decision: largest ever production cut in response to COVID demand collapse
- Initially proposed 1.5 mbd cut in March; Russia refused, triggering price war
- Saudi Arabia flooded market in March (Saudi-Russia price war)
- Market reaction: WTI went negative in April; cuts stabilized prices above $40 by June
- Lesson: OPEC+ failures create dramatic downside; eventual resolution creates sharp recovery

### December 2020 — Gradual easing
- Decision: ease cuts by 500 kbd per month starting January 2021
- Saudi Arabia added voluntary 1 mbd cut in February-March as "gift"
- Market reaction: Brent rose from $50 to $65 over Q1 2021

---

## 2019 Key Decisions

### December 2019 — Deepened cuts
- Decision: additional 500 kbd cut (total to 1.7 mbd) for Q1 2020
- Saudi maintained extra voluntary 400 kbd
- Market reaction: Brent moved from $62 to $65, then collapsed on COVID in March 2020

## OPEC+ Compliance Patterns

### Typical compliance dynamics
- First 2-3 months after new cut: compliance 80-90% (adjustment period)
- Peak compliance: 3-6 months in, typically 90-100%
- Compliance fatigue: after 6+ months, starts eroding
- Key cheaters historically: Iraq, Nigeria, Angola, Kazakhstan
- Most disciplined: Saudi Arabia, UAE (since 2021), Kuwait

### Monitoring compliance
- IEA monthly reports: most comprehensive compliance data (2-month lag)
- OPEC MOMR (Monthly Oil Market Report): OPEC's own estimates (self-reported)
- S&P Global Platts / Argus: tanker tracking-based estimates (near real-time)
- Compliance above 100%: bullish (overcompliance means tighter supply)
- Compliance below 80%: bearish (signals OPEC+ cohesion breaking down)

## Market Reaction Patterns

### Before OPEC+ meetings
- Volatility typically increases 3-5 days before meeting
- Spreads (time and inter-commodity) widen on uncertainty
- Positioning: speculative shorts often cover pre-meeting

### After decisions
- Surprise cuts: immediate $2-6 price jump (depends on magnitude)
- Expected cuts: muted reaction ($0-1), "sell the news" risk
- Rollover/no change: usually bearish if market expected cuts
- Production increases: bearish $1-3 on announcement
- Failed meetings (no deal): most bearish outcome, can trigger $5+ drops

### Duration of impact
- Price impact of cuts typically peaks 2-4 weeks after implementation
- Market reassesses compliance monthly
- Forward curve (backwardation/contango) shifts more durably than flat price
