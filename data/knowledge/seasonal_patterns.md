# Oil Market Seasonal Patterns

Recurring seasonal patterns for Brent crude, Gasoil, and related products.

---

## Q1 (January - March)

### Brent Crude
- Typically weakest quarter for crude demand in the Northern Hemisphere
- Post-holiday demand slowdown; refineries enter planned maintenance (turnarounds)
- OPEC+ often announces output adjustments for H1 around this period
- Historical pattern: Brent tends to trade sideways or pull back from year-end levels
- Exception: geopolitical events (Libya, Middle East) can override seasonal weakness

### Gasoil / Diesel
- Strongest demand period for heating oil (Northern Hemisphere winter)
- Cold snaps in Europe or US can cause sharp Gasoil spikes
- Gasoil crack spread typically peaks in January-February
- Watch: US East Coast heating oil inventories (PADD 1 distillate stocks)
- European gas-to-oil switching during cold weather can add 200-400 kbd demand

### Refinery maintenance
- US Gulf Coast turnarounds begin mid-February to March
- European refineries schedule turnarounds March-April
- Reduced refinery runs -> lower crude demand but tighter product supply

---

## Q2 (April - June)

### Brent Crude
- Spring refinery maintenance peak (April): reduced crude runs
- Post-maintenance ramp-up (May-June): increasing crude demand
- Historically one of the stronger periods as refineries prepare for summer
- "Summer driving season" anticipation builds from May onwards
- OPEC+ June meeting often sets tone for H2

### Gasoil / Diesel
- Post-winter demand dip as heating season ends
- Agricultural diesel demand picks up (spring planting in Northern Hemisphere)
- Crack spread typically narrows from Q1 peak
- Transition period: market shifts focus from heating oil to gasoline

### Gasoline
- Gasoline demand ramps up sharply from Memorial Day (late May) in the US
- Gasoline crack spread widens April-June
- Summer-spec gasoline (lower RVP) more expensive to produce
- Gasoline blending margins drive refinery run decisions

---

## Q3 (July - September)

### Brent Crude
- Peak driving season demand (July-August) supports crude prices
- US crude demand hits annual highs as refineries run at maximum rates
- Hurricane season (June-November, peak August-September) adds risk premium
- Gulf of Mexico production can be disrupted by major hurricanes
- Late Q3: market begins pricing in Q4 demand expectations

### Gasoil / Diesel
- Moderate demand period between seasons
- Pre-winter restocking begins in September (Europe and Asia)
- Gasoil crack spread starts recovering from Q2 lows in September
- Asian demand (China, India) for diesel tends to be strong

### Hurricane impact
- Category 3+ hurricanes in Gulf of Mexico can remove 1-2 mbd temporarily
- Refinery shutdowns in Texas/Louisiana can spike product prices
- Historical examples: Katrina (2005), Harvey (2017), Ida (2021)
- Even near-misses create volatility premium

---

## Q4 (October - December)

### Brent Crude
- Transition from driving season to heating season
- OPEC+ year-end decisions typically announced November-December
- Speculative positioning rebalances before year-end
- "Tax-loss selling" can pressure prices in November-December
- Geopolitical risks often elevated (Middle East tensions, Russia)

### Gasoil / Diesel
- Heating season begins: Gasoil demand increases from October
- Northern Hemisphere winter weather forecasts become key driver
- Gasoil crack spread widens significantly October-December
- European gas storage levels influence gas-to-oil switching potential
- La Nina / El Nino patterns affect winter severity expectations

### Year-end dynamics
- Contango can steepen in December as traders roll positions
- Inventory management by companies ahead of year-end reporting
- Thin liquidity in late December can amplify moves

---

## Cross-Cutting Seasonal Factors

### Chinese demand patterns
- Lunar New Year (January-February): temporary demand dip
- Post-Lunar New Year: strong restocking demand (March-April)
- Golden Week (early October): brief demand pause
- Teapot refinery buying patterns: quota-driven, can distort seasonal norms

### Indian demand patterns
- Monsoon season (June-September): reduced diesel demand for transport
- Post-monsoon festival season: demand recovery October-November
- Rabi crop season (November-March): agricultural diesel demand

### Tanker and logistics seasonality
- Summer: shorter voyage times, lower freight rates
- Winter: weather delays in North Sea, Baltic, higher freight rates
- Suez Canal / Strait of Hormuz transit volumes relatively stable
- Year-end: tanker availability tightens as vessels booked for floating storage
